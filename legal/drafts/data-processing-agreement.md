This Data Processing Agreement ("**DPA**") forms part of, and is incorporated by reference into, the
agreement between the Customer and CLEAR for the provision of the CLEAR platform (the
"**Principal Agreement**" or "**Terms of Service**" / "**ToS**"). It governs CLEAR's processing of
Personal Data on behalf of the Customer.

## 3. Order of precedence

3.1 This DPA forms part of the Principal Agreement.

3.2 In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement / Terms of Service **on
matters of data protection**, **this DPA prevails**.

3.3 In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the **SCCs**, the **SCCs prevail** with respect
to the subject matter they govern.

3.4 Except as expressly modified by this DPA, the Principal Agreement remains in full force and
effect.

## 4. Subject matter, duration, nature and purpose of processing

4.1 **Subject matter.** CLEAR's processing of Customer Personal Data as necessary to provide the
Services under the Principal Agreement, as further described in **Annex I**.

4.2 **Duration.** This DPA applies for the term of the Principal Agreement and for so long as CLEAR
processes Customer Personal Data, plus any period required to comply with the deletion/return
obligations in **Section 12**.

4.3 **Nature and purpose.** Processing operations include collection, storage, organization,
structuring, server-side text extraction from uploaded documents, transmission to the AI engine for
inference, generation and storage of analysis outputs, retrieval, deletion, and otherwise making the
Services available — all for the purpose of enabling the Customer to diagnose and design
behaviour-change interventions using the CLEAR Change Framework.

4.4 The **categories of Data Subjects** and **categories of Personal Data** are set out in
**Annex I**.

## 5. Processing only on documented instructions

5.1 CLEAR shall process Customer Personal Data **only on the Customer's documented instructions**,
including with regard to international transfers, unless required to do so by EU/EEA or Member State
law to which CLEAR is subject. In that case, CLEAR shall inform the Customer of that legal
requirement before processing, **unless that law prohibits such information** on important grounds
of public interest.

5.2 The Customer's documented instructions are constituted by (a) the Principal Agreement, (b) this
DPA, and (c) the Customer's configuration and use of the Services (e.g. creating projects, uploading
documents, inviting respondents, enabling the optional Research agent, deleting projects). Any
additional instructions must be agreed in writing.

5.3 CLEAR shall **immediately inform the Customer** if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes
Data Protection Law. CLEAR is not obliged to perform a legal review of the lawfulness of the
Customer's instructions.

6.1 CLEAR shall ensure that persons authorized to process Customer Personal Data have committed

6.2 CLEAR shall ensure that access to Customer Personal Data is **limited to personnel who need
access** to perform CLEAR's obligations under the Principal Agreement.

## 7. Security of processing (Article 32)

7.1 Taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, and the nature, scope,
context and purposes of processing, as well as the risk to Data Subjects, CLEAR shall implement
appropriate **technical and organizational measures (TOMs)** to ensure a level of security
appropriate to the risk.

7.2 The TOMs in place as at the date of this DPA are described in **Annex II**. CLEAR may update its
measures over time provided the updates do not materially decrease the overall level of security.

7.3 CLEAR shall take steps to ensure that any natural person acting under its authority who has
access to Customer Personal Data does not process it except on the Customer's instructions.

## 8. Assistance with data-subject requests

8.1 Taking into account the nature of the processing, CLEAR shall **assist the Customer** by
appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as possible, in fulfilling the
Customer's obligation to respond to requests by Data Subjects exercising their rights under
Chapter III GDPR (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, and rights
related to automated decision-making).

8.2 The Services provide the Customer (as account owner / project owner) with the ability to
**access, correct, export, and delete** project content and respondent contributions directly. Where
a request cannot be fulfilled through self-service functionality, CLEAR shall provide reasonable
assistance.

8.3 If CLEAR receives a request directly from a Data Subject relating to Customer Personal Data,
CLEAR shall, unless legally prohibited, **promptly forward the request to the Customer** and shall
not respond to it itself except on the Customer's documented instructions.

## 10. Personal Data Breach notification

10.1 CLEAR shall notify the Customer **without undue delay** after becoming aware of a Personal Data
Breach affecting Customer Personal Data.

10.2 The notification shall, to the extent then known and reasonably available, describe: (a) the
nature of the breach, including the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records
concerned; (b) the likely consequences; (c) the measures taken or proposed to address it and
mitigate its effects; and (d) a contact point for further information. Where information is not
available at once, it may be provided in phases without further undue delay.

10.3 CLEAR shall **assist the Customer** in meeting the Customer's own breach-notification
obligations to the Supervisory Authority (Article 33) and to affected Data Subjects (Article 34).
Notifying a Supervisory Authority or Data Subjects remains the **Customer's responsibility** as
Controller.

10.4 Notifications to the Customer shall be sent to `[Customer breach-notification contact]` (or the
account owner's registered email).

# Annex I — Description of the Processing

**A. Categories of Data Subjects**
- The Customer's **target group** (the population whose behaviour the intervention addresses).
- **Stakeholders** named in the challenge brief (names and roles).
- **Respondents** — external stakeholders invited via a tokenized link (`/respond/<token>`) who
  contribute without creating an account.
- The Customer's **own users** (account owner and workspace members).
- Any **third parties** whose Personal Data appears in free-text inputs, uploaded documents, or
  respondent answers.

**B. Categories of Personal Data**
- **Identity / contact data:** names, email addresses, roles (for users, stakeholders, and
  respondents).
- **Free-text inputs:** challenge description, target group, use case, stakeholder lists, timeline —
  **which may contain third parties' Personal Data**.
- **Document contents:** uploaded documents (PDF / DOCX / XLSX / MD / TXT / CSV) and server-extracted
  text — which **may contain employee, customer, financial, or health-related data** controlled by
  the Customer.
- **Respondent answers:** free-text answers, reactions/notes, and optional uploads.
- **Voice-dictation transcripts:** where a user uses the optional browser-local Web Speech API, only
  the **resulting transcript text** is captured; **no audio is recorded or sent** to CLEAR servers.
- **AI outputs:** generated analysis stored against the project.

**C. Special Categories of Data (Article 9) / criminal-offence data (Article 10)**
- **In principle, none.** The Services are **not intended** to process Special Categories of Data.
  The Customer **must not submit** such data (including via free-text fields or uploaded documents)
  **unless it has an appropriate legal basis/condition** under Articles 9/10 — see **Section 13.1(d)**.
  The Customer is the Controller and bears responsibility for any such data it submits.

**D. Frequency of processing**
- Continuous, for the duration of the Principal Agreement (on-demand, driven by Customer use).

**E. Nature and purpose of processing**
- See **Section 4**: hosting, storage, text extraction, transmission to the AI engine for inference,
  generation and storage of analysis, and provision of the Services under the CLEAR Change
  Framework. Optional Research agent may egress web-search queries (server-side only).

**F. Duration / retention** (PROPOSED defaults — confirm with counsel/business)
- **Account + project data:** kept for the life of the account; deleted within `[30]` days of
  account closure; backups purged within `[90]` days.
- **Documents + AI outputs:** same lifecycle as their project; deletable per-project by the owner.
- **Respondent invitations:** invite token expires after **30 days**; contributions retained with
  the project and deletable by the project owner.
- **Billing / accounting records:** retained **7 years** per the Swedish Bookkeeping Act
  (Bokföringslagen). *(Processed by CLEAR as controller; listed here for completeness.)*

# Annex II — Technical and Organizational Measures (Article 32)

CLEAR implements the following measures. *(Measures reflect the current state of the product.
Items noted as gaps are listed transparently and must not be represented as present.)*

**1. Encryption**
- **TLS** encryption of Customer Personal Data **in transit**.
- **Encryption at rest** for the Postgres database (Supabase default).

**2. Access control and tenant isolation**
- **Row-Level Security (RLS)** enforcing tenant isolation across all database tables.
- **JWT-based authentication** for application access.
- Access to Customer Personal Data limited to personnel who need it (see **Section 6**).

**3. Secret and credential management**
- Secrets held **server-side** in Supabase Edge Function secrets.
- **Respondent invite tokens stored hashed (SHA-256)**; tokens expire after 30 days.

**4. Infrastructure and hosting**
- Hosting on **Supabase** (Postgres, Auth, Edge Functions, private document storage) in the
  **EU region** (`eu-central-1` / `eu-west-1`). `[Confirm EU residency.]`
- Private document storage (not publicly accessible).

**5. AI processing controls**
- AI inference via **Anthropic Claude**; **API inputs/outputs not used to train models**; retention
  limited `[confirm zero/limited retention config]`.
- AI and Research-agent calls are **server-side only**, never client-side.
- **Haiku** model used to **de-identify** research findings before any promotion into a shared
  knowledge base reused across projects.

**6. Confidentiality**
- Confidentiality commitments for personnel with access to Customer Personal Data (**Section 6**).

**7. Resilience / backups**
- Database backups provided by the Supabase platform; backup copies purged within `[90]` days of
  deletion. `[Confirm backup, restore, and availability measures.]`

**8. Known gaps (disclosed, not advertised as present)**
- **No application-level audit log.**
- **No documented field-level encryption** beyond Supabase platform defaults.
